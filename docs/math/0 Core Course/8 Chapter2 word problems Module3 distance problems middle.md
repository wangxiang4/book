## 6.Boat traveling in water
- __Downstream speed__: $v_\textsf{down}=v_\textsf{boat}+v_\textsf{current}$
- __Upstream speed__: $v_\textsf{up}=v_\textsf{boat}-v_\textsf{current}$
- __Boat speed__: $v_\textsf{boat}=\frac{v_\textsf{down}+v_\textsf{up}}{2}$
- __Current speed__: $v_\textsf{current}=\frac{v_\textsf{down}-v_\textsf{up}}{2}$


## 7.Focus 5
__The related to the speed of the current__  
- When __a single object moves on water__,
  the time is __affected by the speed of the current__.

### $\fbox{33}\:$A boat moves at $\small 28\textsf{km/h}$ in still water, and the speed of the current is $\small 2\textsf{km/h}$. Find how many hours it will take for the boat to make a round trip between two places $\small 78\textsf{km}$ apart.
::: wrap  
$
\begin{array}{lllll}
\textsf{(A)} \: 5.9 &
\textsf{(B)} \: 5.6 &
\textsf{(C)} \: 5.4 &
\textsf{(D)} \: 4.4 &
\textsf{(E)} \: 4 & \\
\end{array}
$  
:::
#### Solution
::: wrap  
$
\begin{array}{ll}
t=\frac{s}{v_\textsf{boat}+v_\textsf{current}}+\frac{s}{v_\textsf{boat}-v_\textsf{current}} \\
t=\frac{78}{28+2}+\frac{78}{28-2}=2.6+3=5.6 \\
\end{array}
$  
:::  
#### Conclusion
- Derived Solution  
  ::: wrap
  > $\boldsymbol{(B)}$  
  > According to the Solution, get $t=5.6$, so choose $B$. 

  :::
- Formulas used  
  ::: wrap
  >$
  \begin{array}{ll}
  t=\frac{s}{v}\Longrightarrow\frac{s_1}{s_2}=\frac{v_1}{v_2} & \href{/math/0%20Core%20Course/7%20Chapter2%20word%20problems%20Module3%20distance%20problems%20upper.html#basic-formula}{\textsf{straight-line basic formula}} \\

### $\fbox{34}\:$Yacht $\small 1$ and Yacht $\small 2$ travel at speeds of $\small 3.3\textsf{km/h}$ and $\small 2.1\textsf{km/h}$ respectively in still water. The two yachts start at the same time toward each other — yacht $\small 1$ goes upstream, and yacht $\small 2$ goes downstream, with a distance of $\small 27\textsf{km}$ between them. After meeting on the way, yacht $\small 1$ takes another $\small 4$ hours to reach yacht $\small 2\textsf{'s}$ starting point. Find the speed of the current in kilometers per hour.
::: wrap  
$
\begin{array}{lllll}
\textsf{(A)} \: 0.1 &
\textsf{(B)} \: 0.2 &
\textsf{(C)} \: 0.3 &
\textsf{(D)} \: 0.4 &
\textsf{(E)} \: 0.5 & \\
\end{array}
$  
:::
#### Solution
- Show known conditions  
  ![Question distance problems figure Q-34 solve-1.svg](../../public/math/Core%20Course/Question%20distance%20problems%20figure%20Q-34%20solve-1.svg)  
  ::: wrap  
  $
  \begin{array}{ll}
  \textsf{let current/h}=x \\
  \textsf{meeting time}=\frac{27}{\textsf{relative speed}} \\
  t=\frac{27}{3.3-x+2.1+x}=\frac{27}{5.4}=5\textsf{h} \\
  t_{\textsf{yacht}1}=5\textsf{h}+4\textsf{h}=9\textsf{h} \\
  27=9(3.3-x) \\
  27=29.7-9x \\
  9x=2.7 \\
  x=\frac{2.7}{9}=0.3 \\
  \end{array}
  $  
  :::  
#### Conclusion
- Derived Solution  
  ::: wrap
  > $\boldsymbol{(C)}$  
  > According to the Solution, get $x=0.3$, so choose $C$. 

  :::
- Formulas used  
  ::: wrap
  >$
  \begin{array}{ll}
  t=\frac{s}{v}\Longrightarrow\frac{s_1}{s_2}=\frac{v_1}{v_2} & \href{/math/0%20Core%20Course/7%20Chapter2%20word%20problems%20Module3%20distance%20problems%20upper.html#basic-formula}{\textsf{straight-line basic formula}} \\
  v_\textsf{up}=v_\textsf{boat}-v_\textsf{current} & \href{/math/0%20Core%20Course/8%20Chapter2%20word%20problems%20Module3%20distance%20problems%20middle.html#_6-boat-traveling-in-water}{\textsf{upstream speed formula}} \\

## 8.Focus 6
__The not related to the speed of the current__
- When __multiple objects move on water__,
  whether they __meet__ or __pursuit__,
  it is __not affected by the speed of the current__,
  because __the current speed cancels out__.
::: wrap  
- $t_{\textsf{meeting}}=\frac{s}{(v_1+x)+(v_2-x)}$
- $t_{\textsf{pursuit}}=\frac{s}{(v_1+x)-(v_2+x)}=\frac{s}{(v_1-x)-(v_2-x)}$
:::  

### $\fbox{35}\:$Boats $\small 1$ and $\small 2$ are traveling on a river $\small 90$ kilometers apart. If they travel toward each other, they meet after $\small 3$ hours; if they travel in the same direction, Boat $\small 1$ catches up with Boat $\small 2$ after $\small 15$ hours. Find the speed of Boat $\small 1$ in still water in kilometers per hour.
::: wrap  
$
\begin{array}{lllll}
\textsf{(A)} \: 18 &
\textsf{(B)} \: 20 &
\textsf{(C)} \: 22 &
\textsf{(D)} \: 24 &
\textsf{(E)} \: 26 & \\
\end{array}
$  
:::
#### Solution
- Show known conditions  
  ![Question distance problems figure Q-35 solve-1.svg](../../public/math/Core%20Course/Question%20distance%20problems%20figure%20Q-35%20solve-1.svg)  
  ::: wrap  
  $
  \begin{array}{ll}
  \begin{cases}
  90=3(v_{\textsf{boat}1}+v_{\textsf{boat}2}) & \textsf{meeting} \\
  90=15(v_{\textsf{boat}1}-v_{\textsf{boat}2}) & \textsf{pursuit} \\
  \end{cases} \\
  v_{\textsf{boat}1}+v_{\textsf{boat}2}=\frac{90}{3}=30 & ① \\
  v_{\textsf{boat}1}-v_{\textsf{boat}2}=\frac{90}{15}=6 & ② \\
  v_{\textsf{boat}1}+v_{\textsf{boat}2}+v_{\textsf{boat}1}-v_{\textsf{boat}2}=30+6 & ①\,+② \\
  2v_{\textsf{boat}1}=36 \\
  v_{\textsf{boat}1}=\frac{36}{2}=18 \\
  \end{array}
  $  
  :::  
#### Conclusion
- Derived Solution  
  ::: wrap
  > $\boldsymbol{(A)}$  
  > According to the Solution, get $v_{\textsf{boat}1}=18$, so choose $A$. 

  :::
- Formulas used  
  ::: wrap
  >$

  & \href{/math/0%20Core%20Course/8%20Chapter2%20word%20problems%20Module3%20distance%20problems%20middle.html#_8-focus-6}{\textsf{current speed cancellation}} \\
  s=vt \Longrightarrow \frac{s_1}{s_2}=\frac{t_1}{t_2} & \href{/math/0%20Core%20Course/7%20Chapter2%20word%20problems%20Module3%20distance%20problems%20upper.html#basic-formula}{\textsf{straight-line basic formula}} \\

## 9.Relative speed
- __Opposite direction__: $v=v_1+v_2$
- __Same direction__: $v=v_1-v_2$

## 10.Focus 7
__The marching formation__  
- The marching team can be __considered stationary__,
  and __the messenger's movement can be analyzed using relative motion__.

### $\fbox{37}\:$A marching team forms a column $\small 800\textsf{m}$ long, moving at a speed of $\small 80\textsf{m/min}$. A messenger at the front runs to the rear at three times the marching speed, spends $\small 1$ minute delivering a message, and then immediately runs back to the front at the same speed. How long does the messenger spend on the entire round trip $\small \mathpunct{?}$.
::: wrap  
$
\begin{array}{lllll}
\textsf{(A)} \: 6.5\textsf{min} &
\textsf{(B)} \: 7.5\textsf{min} &
\textsf{(C)} \: 8\textsf{min} &
\textsf{(D)} \: 8.5\textsf{min} &
\textsf{(E)} \: 10\textsf{min} & \\

## 11.Focus 8
__The train and pedestrian__
- The pedestrian can be __considered stationary__,
  and __the train can be analyzed using relative motion__.

### $\fbox{38}\:$On a road parallel to a railway, a pedestrian and a cyclist are moving in the same direction. The pedestrian's speed is $\small 3.6\textsf{km/h}$, and the cyclist's speed is $\small 10.8\textsf{km/h}$. A train approaches from behind at a constant speed in the same direction. It takes $\small 22$ seconds to pass the pedestrian and $\small 26$ seconds to pass the cyclist. Find the length of the train in meters.
::: wrap  
$
\begin{array}{lllll}
\textsf{(A)} \: 186 &
\textsf{(B)} \: 268 &
\textsf{(C)} \: 168 &
\textsf{(D)} \: 286 &
\textsf{(E)} \: 188 & \\
\end{array}
$  
:::
#### Solution
- Show known conditions  
  ![Question distance problems figure Q-38 solve-1.svg](../../public/math/Core%20Course/Question%20distance%20problems%20figure%20Q-38%20solve-1.svg)  
  ::: wrap  
  $
  \begin{array}{ll}
  3.6\textsf{km/h}=\frac{3.6\cdot1000}{3600}\textsf{m/s}=1\textsf{m/s} \\
  10.8\textsf{km/h}=\frac{10.8\cdot1000}{3600}\textsf{m/s}=3\textsf{m/s} \\
  \begin{cases}
  l=22(v-1) & \textsf{pedestrian} \\
  l=26(v-3) & \textsf{cyclist} \\
  \end{cases} \\
  l=22(v-1)=26(v-3) \\
  11(v-1)=13(v-3) \\
  11v-11=13v-39 \\
  -2v=-28 \\
  v=\frac{-28}{-2}=14 \\
  l=22(14-1)=22\cdot13=286 & \textsf{substitute }v \\
  \end{array}
  $  
  :::  
#### Conclusion
- Derived Solution  
  ::: wrap
  > $\boldsymbol{(D)}$  
  > According to the Solution, get $l=286$, so choose $D$. 

  :::
- Formulas used  
  ::: wrap
  >$
  \begin{array}{ll}
  s=vt \Longrightarrow \frac{s_1}{s_2}=\frac{t_1}{t_2} & \href{/math/0%20Core%20Course/7%20Chapter2%20word%20problems%20Module3%20distance%20problems%20upper.html#basic-formula}{\textsf{straight-line basic formula}} \\
  v=v_1-v_2 & \href{/math/0%20Core%20Course/8%20Chapter2%20word%20problems%20Module3%20distance%20problems%20middle.html#_9-relative-speed}{\textsf{same-direction relative speed formula}} \\

### $\fbox{39}\:$Two trains, a fast one and a slow one, are $\small 160$ meters and $\small 120$ meters long respectively. They are moving toward each other on parallel tracks. If a passenger on the slow train sees the entire fast train pass by in $\small 4$ seconds, how many seconds will it take for a passenger on the fast train to see the entire slow train pass by $\small \mathpunct{?}$.
::: wrap  
$
\begin{array}{lllll}
\textsf{(A)} \: 3 &
\textsf{(B)} \: 4 &
\textsf{(C)} \: 5 &
\textsf{(D)} \: 6 &
\textsf{(E)} \: 7 & \\
\end{array}
$  
:::
#### Solution
- Show known conditions  
  ![Question distance problems figure Q-39 solve-1.svg](../../public/math/Core%20Course/Question%20distance%20problems%20figure%20Q-39%20solve-1.svg)  
  ::: wrap  
  $
  \begin{array}{ll}
  \begin{cases}
  160=4(v_\textsf{fast}+v_\textsf{slow}) & \textsf{fast} \\
  120=t(v_\textsf{fast}+v_\textsf{slow}) & \textsf{slow} \\
  \end{cases} \\
  v_\textsf{fast}+v_\textsf{slow}=\frac{160}{4}=40 \\
  120=40t \\
  t=\frac{120}{40}=3 \\
  \end{array}
  $  
  :::  
#### Conclusion
- Derived Solution
