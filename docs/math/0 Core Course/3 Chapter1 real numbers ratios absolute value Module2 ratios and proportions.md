## 16.Fractions
- When the unit "$1$" is divided into __several equal parts__, a number that represents __one or several of these parts__ is called __a fraction__.  
  ::: wrap  
  $
  \begin{array}{ll}
  \textsf{fractions}
  \begin{cases}
  \textsf{numerator}\lt\textsf{denominator (e.g. }\frac{3}{7}\textsf{)} & \textsf{proper fraction} \\
  \textsf{numerator}\ge\textsf{denominator (e.g. }\frac{7}{5}\textsf{)} & \textsf{improper fraction} \\
  \end{cases} \\
  \end{array}
  $  
  :::

## 17.Decimals
- __Integer part__
  - __Pure decimal__: a decimal whose integer part is $0$, e.g. $0.21$
  - __Mixed decimal__: a decimal whose integer part is not $0$, e.g. $3.21$
- __Decimal part__
  - __Terminating decimal__: e.g. 0.21
  - __Non-terminating decimal__
    - __Repeating decimal__
      - __Pure repeating decimal__: e.g. $0.212121\cdots$
      - __Mixed repeating decimal__: e.g. $0.3121212\cdots$
    - __Non-repeating decimal__: e.g. $\sqrt{2}, \pi, \mathrm{e}$

## 18.Conversion Between Decimals and Fractions
- Terminating decimal to fraction conversion
  - Use __$\boldsymbol{10, 100, 1000, \cdots}$ as the denominator__.
  - __Example__: $0.21=\frac{21}{100}$
- ###### Pure repeating decimal to fraction conversion
  - Use __$\boldsymbol{9, 99, 999, \cdots}$ as the denominator__,
    where __the number of $\boldsymbol{9\textsf{'s}}$__ equals __the number of repeating digits__.
  - __The numerator is the repeating part__.
  - __Example__: $0.\dot{2}\dot{1}2121\cdots=\frac{21}{99}=\frac{7}{33}$
  - Formula: $0.\underbrace{\dot{a}b\cdots\dot{g}}_{\textsf{repeating digits}}=\frac{ab\cdots g}{\underbrace{99\cdots9}_{9\textsf{'s as repeating digits}}}$
::: wrap  
- ###### Mixed repeating decimal to fraction conversion
  - __The denominator consists of $\boldsymbol{9}\textsf{'s}$ and $\boldsymbol{0}\textsf{'s}$__
    - __the number of $\boldsymbol{9}\textsf{'s}$__ equals __the number of repeating digits__.
    - __the number of $\boldsymbol{0}\textsf{'s}$__ equals __the number of non-repeating digits__.
  - __The numerator__ is obtained by __subtracting the non-repeating part__
    from __the combined number of the non-repeating and repeating parts__.
  - __Example__: $0.3\dot{1}\dot{2}1212\cdots=\frac{312-3}{990}=\frac{309}{990}=\frac{103}{330}$
  - Formula: $0.\underbrace{ab\cdots c}_{m}\underbrace{\dot{d}\cdots\dot{g}}_{n}=\frac{ab\cdots g-ab\cdots c}{\underbrace{99\cdots9}_{m}\underbrace{00\cdots0}_{n}}$
    - $m$: __number of non-repeating digits__ after the decimal point.
    - $n$: __number of repeating digits__.
:::  

## 19.Percentages
- A percentage expresses __how many hundredths one number is of another__,
  and is usually represented by __the symbol "$\boldsymbol{\%}$"__.

# Module 1-02 Ratios and Proportions

## 1.Direct Proportion
- If $\boldsymbol{y=kx} \textsf{ (}k\ne0\textsf{)}$,
  then __$\boldsymbol{y}$ is said to be directly proportional to $\boldsymbol{x}$__,
  and $\boldsymbol{k}$ is called __the constant of proportionality__.  
  ![Direct proportional constant k.svg](../../public/math/Core%20Course/Direct%20proportional%20constant%20k.svg)  
- __Essence__: if __the quotient of two variables__ equals __a nonzero constant__,
  the two variables are __directly proportional__.
- Note: two variables being __directly proportional__ does not mean __they both increase or decrease simultaneously__.
  For example, when $\boldsymbol{k<0}$, __as $\boldsymbol{x}$ increases, $\boldsymbol{y}$ decreases__.

## 2.Inverse Proportion
- If $\boldsymbol{y=\frac{k}{x}} \textsf{ (}k\ne0\textsf{)}$,
  then __$\boldsymbol{y}$ is said to be inversely proportional to $\boldsymbol{x}$__,
  and $\boldsymbol{k}$ is called __the constant of proportionality__.  
  ![Inverse proportional constant k.svg](../../public/math/Core%20Course/Inverse%20proportional%20constant%20k.svg)  
- __Essence__: if __the product of two variables__ equals __a nonzero constant__,
  the two variables are __inversely proportional__.

## 3.Focus 1
__The concept of direct and inverse proportion__  
- If __the quotient of two variables is constant__,
  the two variables are __directly proportional__;
  if __the product of two variables is constant__,
  they are __inversely proportional__.
  Additionally,
  if __$\boldsymbol{y}$ is directly proportional to $\boldsymbol{x}$__,
  then __$\boldsymbol{y}$ is inversely proportional to $\boldsymbol{\frac{1}{x}}$__
  — this illustrates __the mutual conversion between direct and inverse proportion__.

### $\fbox{14}\:$Given that $\small y=y_1-y_2$, where $\small y_1$ is inversely proportional to $\small \frac{1}{2x^2}$ and $\small y_2$ is directly proportional to $\small \frac{3}{x+2}$. When $\small x=0$, $\small y=-3$; and when $\small x=1$, $\small y=1$. Find the expression for $\small y$.
::: wrap  
$
\begin{array}{lllll}
\textsf{(A)} \: y=\frac{3x^2}{2}-\frac{3}{x+2} &
\textsf{(B)} \: y=3x^2-\frac{6}{x+2} &
\textsf{(C)} \: y=3x^2+\frac{6}{x+2} & \\

::: wrap  
$
\begin{array}{ll}
y_1=\frac{k_1}{\frac{1}{2x^2}}=2k_1x^2 \\
y_2=k_2\frac{3}{x+2}=\frac{3k_2}{x+2} \\
y=y_1-y_2=2k_1x^2-\frac{3k_2}{x+2} \\
2k_10^2-\frac{3k_2}{0+2}=-3 & \textsf{substitute }x=0 \\
-\frac{3k_2}{2}=-3 \\

2k_1-2=1 \\
2k_1=3 \\
k_1=\frac{3}{2} \\
y=2\frac{3}{2}x^2-\frac{3\cdot2}{x+2}=3x^2-\frac{6}{x+2} \\
\end{array}

## 5.Proportion
- __Equal ratios are called a proportion__,
  expressed as $\boldsymbol{a:b=c:d}$ or $\boldsymbol{\frac{a}{b}=\frac{c}{d}}$.
  In this proportion,
  __$\boldsymbol{a}$ and $\boldsymbol{d}$ are called the extreme terms__,
  and __$\boldsymbol{b}$ and $\boldsymbol{c}$ are called the mean terms__.

## 6.Mean Proportional (Geometric Mean)
- When $a:b=b:d$, __$\boldsymbol{b}$ is called the mean proportional between $\boldsymbol{a}$ and $\boldsymbol{d}$__.  
  If __$\boldsymbol{a,b,d}$ are all positive numbers__, then __$\boldsymbol{b}$ is the geometric mean of $\boldsymbol{a}$ and $\boldsymbol{d}$__.
  - __Property derivation__  
    ::: wrap  
    $
    \begin{array}{ll}
    \frac{a}{b}=\frac{b}{d} \\
    b^2=ad \\
    b=\pm\sqrt{ad} & ad\ge0 \\
    b=\sqrt[2]{ad} & b \in\mathbb{R_+} \\
    \end{array}
    $  
    :::

## 7.Fundamental Properties of Proportion
- $a:b=c:d \Longrightarrow ad=bc$
- $a:b=b:d \Longrightarrow b^2=ad$

### $\fbox{16}\:\small \frac{4}{5}$ of number $\small A$ is equal to $\small \frac{6}{7}$ of number $\small B$ (both $\small A$ and $\small B$ are nonzero). What is the ratio of $\small A$ to $\small B \,\mathpunct{?}$.

## 9.Focus 4
__The property of proportion__  
- __The product of the extreme terms__ equals __the product of the mean terms__.


### $\fbox{17}\:$In a proportion, the two extreme terms are reciprocals of each other. If one mean term is $\small 2.5$, then what is the other mean term $\small \mathpunct{?}$.
