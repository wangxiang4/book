# Module 1-01 Integers, Fractions, and Decimals

## 1.Integers
- Integers include __positive integers__, __negative integers__, and __zero__, for example: $\cdots, -2, -1, 0, 1, 2, \cdots$  
  $
  \begin{array}{ll}
  \mathbb{Z}
  \begin{cases}
  \mathbb{Z_+} & \textsf{(positive integers)} \\
  0 & \textsf{(zero)} \\
  \mathbb{Z_-} & \textsf{(negative integers)} \\
  \end{cases} \\
  \end{array}
  $  
- Positive integers and zero are called __non-negative integers__.

## 2.Natural Numbers
- $\mathbb{N}=0, 1, 2, \cdots$
- Note: natural numbers include __positive integers__ and __zero__. The smallest natural number is $\boldsymbol{0}$.

### $\fbox{2}\:$(Data Sufficiency) $\small m$ is an integer.
::: wrap  
$
\begin{array}{lllll}
\textsf{(1)} \: m=\frac{p}{q}\textsf{, where }p\textsf{ and }q\textsf{ are nonzero integers, and }m^2\textsf{ is an integer} & \\
\textsf{(2)} \: m=\frac{p}{q}\textsf{, where }p\textsf{ and }q\textsf{ are nonzero integers, and }\frac{2m+4}{3}\textsf{ is an integer} & \\
\end{array}
$  
$
\begin{array}{lllll}
\textsf{(A)} \: \textsf{(1)} &
\textsf{(B)} \: \textsf{(2)} & \\
\textsf{(C)} \: \textsf{(1)}+\textsf{(2)} &  
\textsf{(D)} \: \textsf{(1)},\textsf{(2)} & \\
\textsf{(E)} \: \varnothing & \\
\end{array}
$  
:::
#### Solution
::: wrap  
$
\begin{array}{ll}
\textcolor{#9333EA}{\textsf{verify condition }\textsf{(1)}} \\
m=\frac{p}{q}\textsf{ (rational fraction)} \\
m^2=a \longrightarrow m=\pm\sqrt{a} & a\ge0 \\
a=\textsf{perfect square} \Longrightarrow m\in\mathbb{Z} & a\in\mathbb{Z} \land m=\frac{p}{q} \\
\textcolor{#9333EA}{\textsf{verify condition }\textsf{(2)}} \\
m=\frac{p}{q}\textsf{ (rational fraction)} \\
\textsf{let }m=\frac{5}{2} \\
\frac{2\frac{5}{2}+4}{3}=3 \\
m=\frac{5}{2}=2.5 \Longrightarrow m\notin\mathbb{Z} \\
\end{array}
$  
:::
#### Conclusion
- Derived Solution  
  ::: wrap

  > According to the Solution, get $\textsf{(1)}\,✅\;,\textsf{(2)}\,❌\;$, so choose $A$.

## 4.Odd Numbers
- Numbers that are __not divisible by $\boldsymbol{2}$__;
  they can be expressed as $\boldsymbol{2k+1}\lor\boldsymbol{2k-1}$,
  where $\boldsymbol{k}$ is __an integer__.

## 5.Even Numbers
- Numbers that are __divisible by $\boldsymbol{2}$__;
  they can be expressed as $\boldsymbol{2k}$,
  where $\boldsymbol{k}$ is __an integer__.


## 6.Parity Combination Properties

|                                              |                                                |                                                 |
|:--------------------------------------------:|:----------------------------------------------:|:-----------------------------------------------:|
| $\textsf{odd}\pm\textsf{odd}=\textsf{even}$  |  $\textsf{odd}\pm\textsf{even}=\textsf{odd}$   |  $\textsf{even}\pm\textsf{even}=\textsf{even}$  |
| $\textsf{odd}\cdot\textsf{odd}=\textsf{odd}$ | $\textsf{odd}\cdot\textsf{even}=\textsf{even}$ | $\textsf{even}\cdot\textsf{even}=\textsf{even}$ |
- Note: $\boldsymbol{0}$ is __an even number__, and __any two consecutive integers__ are __one odd__ and __one even__.

\textsf{let } \textsf{odd}\gt \textsf{even}\gt \textsf{odd} \\

## 9.Prime Numbers
- A positive integer __greater than $\boldsymbol{1}$__ that can be __divided only by $\boldsymbol{1}$ and itself__ is called __a prime number__.  
  $2, 3, 5, 7, \cdots$

## 10.Composite Numbers
- A positive integer that can be __divided by $\boldsymbol{1}$__, __itself__,
  __and at least one other positive integer__ is called __a composite number__.  
  $4, 6, 8, 9, \cdots$

## 11.Important Properties
1. Both prime and composite numbers are within __the set of positive integers__,
   and there are __infinitely many of them__.
   __The number $\boldsymbol{1}$ is neither prime nor composite__.
2. __The number $\boldsymbol{2}$ is both a prime and an even integer__,
   known as __the even prime__.
   Every prime number __greater than $\boldsymbol{2}$ is odd__.
   Among all primes, __$\boldsymbol{2}$ is the only even and smallest one__.
3. __The smallest composite number is $\boldsymbol{4}$__.
   A positive integer that can be __expressed as the product of two or more prime numbers__ is called __a composite number__.
4. If __the sum or difference of two prime numbers is odd__,
   then __one of them must be $\boldsymbol{2}$__.
   If __the product of two prime numbers is even__,
   then __one of them must also be $\boldsymbol{2}$__.

## 12.Coprime Numbers
- Two numbers whose __greatest common divisor is $\boldsymbol{1}$__ are called __coprime numbers__.  
  $\frac{4}{9}$ (since $4$ and $9$ __share no common factors other than $\boldsymbol{1}$__).
- Note: two numbers do not have to be __prime themselves__ to be __coprime__.

### $\fbox{7}\:$Using the prime numbers less than $\small 10$ to form a three-digit number without repeating digits, make the number divisible by both $\small 3$ and $\small 5$. If the smallest such number is $\small m$ and the largest is $\small n$, find $\small n-m$.

\end{array}
$  
:::
