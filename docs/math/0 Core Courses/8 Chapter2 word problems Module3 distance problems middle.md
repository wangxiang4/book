## 6.Boat traveling in water
- __Downstream speed__: $v_\textsf{down}=v_\textsf{boat}+v_\textsf{current}$
- __Upstream speed__: $v_\textsf{up}=v_\textsf{boat}-v_\textsf{current}$
- __Boat speed__: $v_\textsf{boat}=\frac{v_\textsf{down}+v_\textsf{up}}{2}$
- __Current speed__: $v_\textsf{current}=\frac{v_\textsf{down}-v_\textsf{up}}{2}$


## 7.Focus 5
__The related to the speed of the current__  
- When __a single object moves on water__,
  the time is __affected by the speed of the current__.

### $\fbox{33}\:$A boat moves at $\small 28\textsf{km/h}$ in still water, and the speed of the current is $\small 2\textsf{km/h}$. Find how many hours it will take for the boat to make a round trip between two places $\small 78\textsf{km}$ apart.
::: wrap  
$
\begin{array}{lllll}
\textsf{(A)} \: 5.9 &
\textsf{(B)} \: 5.6 &
\textsf{(C)} \: 5.4 &
\textsf{(D)} \: 4.4 &
\textsf{(E)} \: 4 & \\
\end{array}
$  
:::
#### Solution
::: wrap  
$
\begin{array}{ll}
t=\frac{s}{v_\textsf{boat}+v_\textsf{current}}+\frac{s}{v_\textsf{boat}-v_\textsf{current}} \\
t=\frac{78}{28+2}+\frac{78}{28-2}=2.6+3=5.6 \\
\end{array}
$  
:::  
#### Conclusion
- Derived Solution  
  ::: wrap
  > $\boldsymbol{(B)}$  
  > According to the Solution, get $t=5.6$, so choose $B$. 

  :::
- Formulas used  
  ::: wrap
  >$
  \begin{array}{ll}
  t=\frac{s}{v}\Longrightarrow\frac{s_1}{s_2}=\frac{v_1}{v_2} & \href{/math/0%20Core%20Courses/7%20Chapter2%20word%20problems%20Module3%20distance%20problems%20upper.html#basic-formula}{\textsf{straight-line basic formula}} \\

### $\fbox{34}\:$Yacht $\small 1$ and Yacht $\small 2$ travel at speeds of $\small 3.3\textsf{km/h}$ and $\small 2.1\textsf{km/h}$ respectively in still water. The two yachts start at the same time toward each other — yacht $\small 1$ goes upstream, and yacht $\small 2$ goes downstream, with a distance of $\small 27\textsf{km}$ between them. After meeting on the way, yacht $\small 1$ takes another $\small 4$ hours to reach yacht $\small 2\textsf{'s}$ starting point. Find the speed of the current in kilometers per hour.
::: wrap  
$
\begin{array}{lllll}
\textsf{(A)} \: 0.1 &
\textsf{(B)} \: 0.2 &
\textsf{(C)} \: 0.3 &
\textsf{(D)} \: 0.4 &
\textsf{(E)} \: 0.5 & \\
\end{array}
$  
:::
#### Solution
- Show known conditions  
  ![Question distance problems figure Q-34 solve-1.svg](../../public/math/Core%20Courses/Question%20distance%20problems%20figure%20Q-34%20solve-1.svg)  
  ::: wrap  
  $
  \begin{array}{ll}
  \textsf{let current/h}=x \\
  \textsf{meeting time}=\frac{27}{\textsf{relative speed}} \\
  t=\frac{27}{3.3-x+2.1+x}=\frac{27}{5.4}=5\textsf{h} \\
  t_{\textsf{yacht}1}=5\textsf{h}+4\textsf{h}=9\textsf{h} \\
  27=9(3.3-x) \\
  27=29.7-9x \\
  9x=2.7 \\
  x=\frac{2.7}{9}=0.3 \\
  \end{array}
  $  
  :::  
#### Conclusion
- Derived Solution  
  ::: wrap
  > $\boldsymbol{(C)}$  
  > According to the Solution, get $x=0.3$, so choose $C$. 

  :::
- Formulas used  
  ::: wrap
  >$
  \begin{array}{ll}
  t=\frac{s}{v}\Longrightarrow\frac{s_1}{s_2}=\frac{v_1}{v_2} & \href{/math/0%20Core%20Courses/7%20Chapter2%20word%20problems%20Module3%20distance%20problems%20upper.html#basic-formula}{\textsf{straight-line basic formula}} \\
  v_\textsf{up}=v_\textsf{boat}-v_\textsf{current} & \href{/math/0%20Core%20Courses/8%20Chapter2%20word%20problems%20Module3%20distance%20problems%20middle.html#_6-boat-traveling-in-water}{\textsf{upstream speed formula}} \\
  v_\textsf{down}=v_\textsf{boat}+v_\textsf{current} & \href{/math/0%20Core%20Courses/8%20Chapter2%20word%20problems%20Module3%20distance%20problems%20middle.html#_6-boat-traveling-in-water}{\textsf{downstream speed formula}} \\

## 8.Focus 6
__The not related to the speed of the current__
- When __multiple objects move on water__,
  whether they __meet__ or __pursuit__,
  it is __not affected by the speed of the current__,
  because __the current speed cancels out__.
- $t_{\textsf{meeting}}=\frac{s}{(v_1+x)+(v_2-x)}$
- $t_{\textsf{pursuit}}=\frac{s}{(v_1+x)-(v_2+x)}=\frac{s}{(v_1-x)-(v_2-x)}$

### $\fbox{35}\:$Boats $\small 1$ and $\small 2$ are traveling on a river $\small 90$ kilometers apart. If they travel toward each other, they meet after $\small 3$ hours; if they travel in the same direction, Boat $\small 1$ catches up with Boat $\small 2$ after $\small 15$ hours. Find the speed of Boat $\small 1$ in still water in kilometers per hour.
::: wrap  
$
\begin{array}{lllll}
\textsf{(A)} \: 18 &
\textsf{(B)} \: 20 &
\textsf{(C)} \: 22 &
\textsf{(D)} \: 24 &
\textsf{(E)} \: 26 & \\
\end{array}
$  
:::
#### Solution
- Show known conditions  
  ![Question distance problems figure Q-35 solve-1.svg](../../public/math/Core%20Courses/Question%20distance%20problems%20figure%20Q-35%20solve-1.svg)  
  ::: wrap  
  $
  \begin{array}{ll}
  \begin{cases}
  90=3(v_{\textsf{boat}1}+v_{\textsf{boat}2}) & \textsf{meeting} \\
  90=15(v_{\textsf{boat}1}-v_{\textsf{boat}2}) & \textsf{pursuit} \\
  \end{cases} \\
  v_{\textsf{boat}1}+v_{\textsf{boat}2}=\frac{90}{3}=30 & ① \\
  v_{\textsf{boat}1}-v_{\textsf{boat}2}=\frac{90}{15}=6 & ② \\
  v_{\textsf{boat}1}+v_{\textsf{boat}2}+v_{\textsf{boat}1}-v_{\textsf{boat}2}=30+6 & ①\,+② \\
  2v_{\textsf{boat}1}=36 \\
  v_{\textsf{boat}1}=\frac{36}{2}=18 \\
  \end{array}
  $  
  :::  
#### Conclusion
- Derived Solution  
  ::: wrap
  > $\boldsymbol{(A)}$  
  > According to the Solution, get $v_{\textsf{boat}1}=18$, so choose $A$. 

  :::

  ::: wrap
  >$

  & \href{/math/0%20Core%20Courses/8%20Chapter2%20word%20problems%20Module3%20distance%20problems%20middle.html#_8-focus-6}{\textsf{current speed cancellation}} \\

### $\fbox{36}\:$A boat set out upstream at 8:00 AM (let the boat speed and the current speed be constant). Partway through the trip, a plank fell off the boat into the water. The crew didn't notice the loss until 8:50 AM, when they immediately turned the boat around to chase it. They finally caught up with the plank at 9:20 AM. At what time did the plank fall into the water $\small \mathpunct{?}$.
::: wrap  
$
\begin{array}{lllll}
\textsf{(A)} \: \textsf{8:35 AM} &
\textsf{(B)} \: \textsf{8:30 AM} &
\textsf{(C)} \: \textsf{8:25 AM} &
\textsf{(D)} \: \textsf{8:20 AM} &
\textsf{(E)} \: \textsf{8:15 AM} & \\
\end{array}
$  
:::
#### Solution
- Show known conditions  
  ![Question distance problems figure Q-36 solve-1.svg](../../public/math/Core%20Courses/Question%20distance%20problems%20figure%20Q-36%20solve-1.svg)  
  ::: wrap  
  $
  \begin{array}{ll}
  \textsf{current speed}=0 \\
  \textsf{9:20}-\textsf{8:50}=30 \\
  \textsf{8:50}-30=\textsf{8:20} \\
  \end{array}
  $  
  :::  
#### Conclusion
- Derived Solution  
  ::: wrap
  > $\boldsymbol{(D)}$  
  > According to the Solution, get $\textsf{8:20}$, so choose $D$.
