# Module 2-03 Distance Problems

## 1.Linear Distance
### $\textcolor{#1E3A5F}{\fbox{1}}\:$Basic formula
- $s=vt$  
  $\boldsymbol{\frac{s_1}{s_2}=\frac{t_1}{t_2}}$: $s$ is in [direct proportion](/math/0%20Core%20Courses/3%20Chapter1%20real%20numbers-ratios-absolute%20values%20Module2%20ratios%20and%20proportions.html#_1-direct-proportion) to $t$,
  where $v$ is __the constant of proportionality__.
- $v=\frac{s}{t}$  
  $\boldsymbol{\frac{v_1}{v_2}=\frac{t_2}{t_1}}$: $v$ is in [inverse proportion](/math/0%20Core%20Courses/3%20Chapter1%20real%20numbers-ratios-absolute%20values%20Module2%20ratios%20and%20proportions.html#_2-inverse-proportion) to $t$,
  where $s$ is __the constant of proportionality__.
- $t=\frac{s}{v}$  
  $\boldsymbol{\frac{s_1}{s_2}=\frac{v_1}{v_2}}$: $s$ is in [direct proportion](/math/0%20Core%20Courses/3%20Chapter1%20real%20numbers-ratios-absolute%20values%20Module2%20ratios%20and%20proportions.html#_1-direct-proportion) to $v$,
  where $t$ is __the constant of proportionality__.

### $\textcolor{#1E3A5F}{\fbox{2}}\:$Head-on meeting formula
- $s_{\textsf{meeting}}=s_1+s_2=v_1t+v_2t=(v_1+v_2)t$
- __Formula derivation__  
  ![Head-on meeting formula derivation.svg](../../public/math/Core%20Courses/Head-on%20meeting%20formula%20derivation.svg)  
  ::: wrap  
  $
  \begin{array}{ll}
  s=s_1+s_2=v_1t+v_2t=(v_1+v_2)t \\
  t=\frac{\textsf{sum of distances}}{\textsf{sum of speeds}}=\frac{s_1+s_2}{v_1+v_2} \\
  \end{array}
  $  
  :::

### $\textcolor{#1E3A5F}{\fbox{3}\:}\:$Pursuit formula
- $s_{\textsf{pursuit}}=s_1-s_2=v_1t-v_2t=(v_1-v_2)t$
- __Same time__: $\boldsymbol{t=\frac{s_1}{v_1}=\frac{s_2}{v_2}=\frac{s_1+s_2}{v_1+v_2}=\frac{s_1-s_2}{v_1-v_2}}$
- __Formula derivation__  
  ![Pursuit formula derivation.svg](../../public/math/Core%20Courses/Pursuit%20formula%20derivation.svg)  
  ::: wrap  
  $
  \begin{array}{ll}
  s=s_1-s_2=v_1t-v_2t=(v_1-v_2)t \\
  t=\frac{\textsf{difference of distances}}{\textsf{difference of speeds}}=\frac{s_1-s_2}{v_1-v_2} \\
  \end{array}
  $  
  :::


## 2.Focus 1
__The straight-line meeting__  
- Two vehicles __move toward each other__. 
- $\textsf{meeting time}=\frac{\textsf{sum of distances}}{\textsf{sum of speeds}}$
- $\textsf{meeting time}=\frac{\textsf{difference of distances}}{\textsf{difference of speeds}}$

  
### $\fbox{25}\:$Cars $\small 1$ and $\small 2$ set out at the same time from the east and west ends, heading toward each other. Car $\small 1$ travels at $\small 56\textsf{km/h}$, and car $\small 2$ at $\small 48\textsf{km/h}$. They meet $\small 32\textsf{km}$ away from the midpoint of the route. Find the distance between the eastern and western locations.
::: wrap  
$
\begin{array}{lllll}
\textsf{(A)} \: 799\textsf{km} &
\textsf{(B)} \: 810\textsf{km} &
\textsf{(C)} \: 832\textsf{km} &
\textsf{(D)} \: 850\textsf{km} &
\textsf{(E)} \: 883\textsf{km} & \\
\end{array}
$  
:::
#### Solution
- Show known conditions  
  ![Question distance problems figure Q-25 solve-1.svg](../../public/math/Core%20Courses/Question%20distance%20problems%20figure%20Q-25%20solve-1.svg)  
  ::: wrap  
  $
  \begin{array}{ll}
  s_{\textsf{difference}}=s_{\textsf{car1}}-s_{\textsf{car2}}=(\frac{s}{2}+32)-(\frac{s}{2}-32)=64 \\
  t=\frac{s_{\textsf{difference}}}{v_{\textsf{difference}}}=\frac{32\cdot2}{56-48}=\frac{64}{8}=8\textsf{h} \\
  s=(v_1+v_2)t=(56+48)8=104\cdot8=832 \\
  \end{array}
  $   
  :::
#### Conclusion
- Derived Solution  
  ::: wrap
  > $\boldsymbol{(C)}$  
  > According to the Solution, get $s=832$, so choose $C$. 

  :::
- Formulas used  
  ::: wrap
  >$
  \begin{array}{ll}
  t=\frac{s}{v}\Longrightarrow\frac{s_1}{s_2}=\frac{v_1}{v_2} & \href{/math/0%20Core%20Courses/7%20Chapter2%20word%20problems%20Module3%20distance%20problems%20upper.html#basic-formula}{\textsf{linear basic formula}} \\
  s_{\textsf{meeting}}=s_1+s_2=v_1t+v_2t=(v_1+v_2)t & \href{/math/0%20Core%20Courses/7%20Chapter2%20word%20problems%20Module3%20distance%20problems%20upper.html#head-on-meeting-formula}{\textsf{linear head-on meeting formula}} \\
  \end{array}

  :::

### $\fbox{26}\:$Car $\small 1$ and Car $\small 2$ start at the same time from points $\small A$ and $\small B$ respectively, traveling toward each other. Car $\small 1$ travels at $\small 80\textsf{km/h}$, and Car $\small 2$ travels at $\small 10\%$ of the total distance per hour. When Car $\small 2$ has covered $\small \frac{5}{8}$ of the total distance, Car $\small 1$ still needs to travel $\small \frac{1}{6}$ of the total distance to reach point $\small B$. Find the distance between $\small A$ and $\small B$.
::: wrap  
$
\begin{array}{lllll}
\textsf{(A)} \: 400\textsf{km} &
\textsf{(B)} \: 450\textsf{km} &
\textsf{(C)} \: 500\textsf{km} &
\textsf{(D)} \: 550\textsf{km} &
\textsf{(E)} \: 600\textsf{km} & \\
\end{array}
$  
:::
#### Solution
- Show known conditions  
  ![Question distance problems figure Q-26 solve-1.svg](../../public/math/Core%20Courses/Question%20distance%20problems%20figure%20Q-26%20solve-1.svg)  
  ::: wrap  
  $
  \begin{array}{ll}
  \frac{s_{\textsf{car}1}}{s_{\textsf{car}2}}=\frac{v_{\textsf{car}1}}{v_{\textsf{car}2}} & t=\textsf{constant} \\
  \frac{s\frac{5}{6}}{s\frac{5}{8}}=\frac{80}{0.1s}=\frac{4}{3} \\
  0.4s=240 \\
  s=\frac{240}{0.4}=600 \\
  \end{array}
  $   
  :::
#### Conclusion
- Derived Solution  
  ::: wrap
  > $\boldsymbol{(E)}$  
  > According to the Solution, get $s=600$, so choose $E$. 

  :::
- Formulas used  
  ::: wrap
  >$
  \begin{array}{ll}
  t=\frac{s}{v}\Longrightarrow\frac{s_1}{s_2}=\frac{v_1}{v_2} & \href{/math/0%20Core%20Courses/7%20Chapter2%20word%20problems%20Module3%20distance%20problems%20upper.html#basic-formula}{\textsf{linear basic formula}} \\
  \end{array}
  >$

  :::

## 3.Focus 2
__The straight-line round-trip meeting__  
- For __multiple round-trip meetings__,
  set up equations based on __the relationship between their distances__.
- __Straight-line round-trip discriminant__  
  - $\boldsymbol{\frac{v_{\textsf{fast}}}{s_\textsf{slow}}=2}$  
    ![Discriminant cases of straight-line round-trip graph 1.svg](../../public/math/Core%20Courses/Discriminant%20cases%20of%20straight-line%20round-trip%20graph%201.svg)  
    ::: wrap  
    $
    \begin{array}{ll}
    \textsf{sum of distances}_\textsf{first meeting}=s_{a}+s_{b}=s \\
    \textsf{sum of distances}_\textsf{second meeting}=s_{a}+s_{b}=2s+s=3s \\
    \end{array}
    $
    :::  

  - $\frac{v_{\textsf{fast}}}{s_\textsf{slow}}\lt2$  
    ![Discriminant cases of straight-line round-trip graph 2.svg](../../public/math/Core%20Courses/Discriminant%20cases%20of%20straight-line%20round-trip%20graph%202.svg)  
    ::: wrap  
    $
    \begin{array}{ll}
    \textsf{sum of distances}_\textsf{first meeting}=s_{a}+s_{b}=s \\
    \textsf{sum of distances}_\textsf{second meeting}=s_{a}+s_{b}=3s \\
    \end{array}
    $
    :::

  - $\frac{v_{\textsf{fast}}}{s_\textsf{slow}}\gt2$  
    ![Discriminant cases of straight-line round-trip graph 3.svg](../../public/math/Core%20Courses/Discriminant%20cases%20of%20straight-line%20round-trip%20graph%203.svg)  
    ::: wrap  
    $
    \begin{array}{ll}
    \textsf{sum of distances}_\textsf{first meeting}=s_{a}+s_{b}=s \\
    \textsf{sum of distances}_\textsf{second meeting}=s_{a}-s_{b}=s \\
    \end{array}
    $
    :::


### $\fbox{27}\:$Cars $\small 1$ and $\small 2$ depart from the East Station to the West Station at the same time. Car $\small 1$ travels $\small 12\textsf{km}$ per hour faster than Car $\small 2$. After driving for four and a half hours to reach the West Station, Car $\small 1$ immediately returns along the same route and meets Car $\small 2$ at a point $\small 31.5\textsf{km}$ from the West Station. Find the speed of Car $\small 1$ in kilometers per hour.
::: wrap  
$
\begin{array}{lllll}
\textsf{(A)} \: 42 &
\textsf{(B)} \: 50 &
\textsf{(C)} \: 55 &
\textsf{(D)} \: 56 &
\textsf{(E)} \: 60 & \\
\end{array}
$  
:::
#### Solution
- Show known conditions  
  ![Question distance problems figure Q-27 solve-1.svg](../../public/math/Core%20Courses/Question%20distance%20problems%20figure%20Q-27%20solve-1.svg)     
  ::: wrap  
  $
  \begin{array}{ll}
  t=\frac{s_{\textsf{difference}}}{v_{\textsf{difference}}}=\frac{2\cdot31.5}{v+12-v}=\frac{63}{12}=\frac{21}{4}=5.25\textsf{h} \\
  \textsf{car1: }t_{BC}=5.25-4.5=0.75 \\
  v_{\textsf{car1}}=\frac{31.5}{0.75}=\frac{31.5}{\frac{3}{4}}=\frac{31.5\cdot4}{3}=42 \\
  \end{array}
  $   
  :::
#### Conclusion
- Derived Solution  
  ::: wrap
  > $\boldsymbol{(A)}$  
  > According to the Solution, get $v_{\textsf{car1}}=42$, so choose $A$. 

  :::
- Formulas used  
  ::: wrap
  >$
  \begin{array}{ll}
  t=\frac{s}{v}\Longrightarrow\frac{s_1}{s_2}=\frac{v_1}{v_2} & \href{/math/0%20Core%20Courses/7%20Chapter2%20word%20problems%20Module3%20distance%20problems%20upper.html#basic-formula}{\textsf{linear basic formula}} \\
  v=\frac{s}{t}\Longrightarrow\frac{v_1}{v_2}=\frac{t_2}{t_1} & \href{/math/0%20Core%20Courses/7%20Chapter2%20word%20problems%20Module3%20distance%20problems%20upper.html#basic-formula}{\textsf{linear basic formula}} \\
  \end{array}
