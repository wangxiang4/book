# Module 2-01 Proportions and Percentages

## 1.Focus 1
__The fixed ratio problems (given total quantity, find partial quantity)__  
- The standard method is to __set unknown variables and solve__,
  while __a more concise method is to use the formula__:
  $\textsf{partial quantity}=\textsf{total quantity}\cdot\textsf{corresponding ratio}$

### $\fbox{1}\:$A company borrows $\small 340\textsf{K USD}$ from the bank and distributes it to its three departments ($\small A,B$, and $\small C$) for technical upgrades according to the ratio $\small \frac{1}{2}:\frac{1}{3}:\frac{1}{9}$. How much does department $\small A$ receive $\small \mathpunct{?}$.
::: wrap  
$
\begin{array}{lllll}
\textsf{(A)} \: 170\textsf{K USD} &
\textsf{(B)} \: 80\textsf{K USD} &
\textsf{(C)} \: 120\textsf{K USD} & \\
\textsf{(D)} \: 180\textsf{K USD} &
\textsf{(E)} \: 190\textsf{K USD} & \\
\end{array}
$  
:::
#### Solution
::: wrap  
$
\begin{array}{ll}
A:B:C=\frac{1}{2}:\frac{1}{3}:\frac{1}{9}=9:6:2 & \textsf{lcm}=18 \\
A=\frac{9}{9+6+2}\cdot34=\frac{306}{17}=18 \\
\end{array}
$  
:::  
#### Conclusion
- Derived Solution  
  ::: wrap
  > $\boldsymbol{(D)}$  
  > According to the Solution, get $A=18$, so choose $D$. 

  :::
- Formulas used  
  ::: wrap
  >$
  \begin{array}{ll}
  \textsf{partial quantity}=\textsf{total quantity}\cdot\textsf{corresponding ratio} & \href{/math/0%20Core%20Courses/5%20Chapter2%20word%20problems%20Module1%20proportions%20and%20percentages.html#_1-focus-1}{\textsf{proportion formula}} \\
  \end{array}
  >$

  :::

## 2.Focus 2
__The fixed ratio problems (given partial quantity, find total quantity)__
- The standard method is to __set unknown variables and solve__,
  while __a more concise method is to use the formula__:
  $\textsf{total quantity}=\frac{\textsf{partial quantity}}{\textsf{corresponding ratio}}$

### $\fbox{2}\:$Use $\small \frac{5}{8}$ of a sum of money to buy product $\small A$, then use $\small \frac{2}{5}$ of the remaining amount to buy product $\small B$, and finally $\small 900\textsf{ USD}$ is left. What was the total amount of money originally $\small \mathpunct{?}$.
::: wrap  
$
\begin{array}{lllll}
\textsf{(A)} \: 2.4\textsf{K USD} &
\textsf{(B)} \: 3.6\textsf{K USD} &
\textsf{(C)} \: 4\textsf{K USD} & \\
\textsf{(D)} \: 4.5\textsf{K USD} &
\textsf{(E)} \: 5\textsf{K USD} & \\
\end{array}
$  
:::
#### Solution
::: wrap  
$
\begin{array}{ll}
\textsf{total quantity}=\frac{900}{1-\frac{5}{8}-\frac{3}{8}\cdot\frac{2}{5}} \\
\frac{900}{\frac{3}{8}-\frac{3}{8}\cdot\frac{2}{5}}=\frac{900}{\frac{3}{8}(1-\frac{2}{5})}=\frac{900}{\frac{3}{8}\cdot\frac{3}{5}} \\
\frac{900}{1}\cdot\frac{40}{9}=\frac{36000}{9}=4000 \\
\end{array}
$  
:::  
#### Conclusion
- Derived Solution  
  ::: wrap
  > $\boldsymbol{(C)}$  
  > According to the Solution, get $\textsf{total quantity}=4000$, so choose $C$. 

  :::
- Formulas used  
  ::: wrap
  >$
  \begin{array}{ll}
  \textsf{total quantity}=\frac{\textsf{partial quantity}}{\textsf{corresponding ratio}} & \href{/math/0%20Core%20Courses/5%20Chapter2%20word%20problems%20Module1%20proportions%20and%20percentages.html#_2-focus-2}{\textsf{proportion formula}} \\
  \end{array}
  >$

### $\fbox{3}\:$A bonus is distributed among $\small A, B, C$, and $\small D$. $\small \frac{1}{5}$ of the total is given to $\small A$, $\small \frac{1}{3}$ of the total is given to $\small B$, the amount given to $\small C$ is three times the difference between $\small A\textsf{'s}$ and $\small B\textsf{'s}$ bonuses, and $\small D$ receives $\small 200\textsf{ USD}$. Find the total amount of the bonus.
::: wrap  
$
\begin{array}{lllll}
\textsf{(A)} \: 1.5\textsf{K USD} &
\textsf{(B)} \: 2\textsf{K USD} &
\textsf{(C)} \: 2.5\textsf{K USD} & \\
\textsf{(D)} \: 3\textsf{K USD} &
\textsf{(E)} \: 5\textsf{K USD} & \\
\end{array}
$  
:::
#### Solution
::: wrap  
$
\begin{array}{ll}
\textsf{total quantity}=\frac{200}{1-\frac{1}{5}-\frac{1}{3}-(\frac{1}{3}-\frac{1}{5})\cdot3} \\
\frac{200}{\frac{15}{15}-\frac{3}{15}-\frac{5}{15}-(\frac{5}{15}-\frac{3}{15})\cdot3} \\
\frac{200}{\frac{15}{15}-\frac{3}{15}-\frac{5}{15}-\frac{6}{15}} \\
\frac{200}{\frac{15}{15}-\frac{14}{15}}=\frac{200}{\frac{1}{15}} \\
\frac{200}{1}\cdot\frac{15}{1}=3000 \\
\end{array}
$  
:::
#### Conclusion
- Derived Solution  
  ::: wrap
  > $\boldsymbol{(D)}$  
  > According to the Solution, get $\textsf{total quantity}=3000$, so choose $D$.

  :::
- Formulas used  
  ::: wrap
  >$
  \begin{array}{ll}
  \textsf{total quantity}=\frac{\textsf{partial quantity}}{\textsf{corresponding ratio}} & \href{/math/0%20Core%20Courses/5%20Chapter2%20word%20problems%20Module1%20proportions%20and%20percentages.html#_2-focus-2}{\textsf{proportion formula}} \\

  >$

## 3.Focus 3
__The fixed ratio problems (given partial quantity, find partial quantity)__
- First,
  identify __the intermediary quantity that links the parts__,
  then __set up an unknown or an equation to solve__.
  

### $\fbox{4}\:$A department has $\small 420$ male employees, and the number of male employees is $\small 1\frac{1}{3}$ times the number of female employees. Those with more than $\small 20$ years of service make up $\small 20\%$ of all employees. Employees with from $\small 10$ to $\small 20$ years of service are half the number of those with less than $\small 10$ years of service. How many employees have less than $\small 10$ years of service $\small \mathpunct{?}$.
::: wrap  
$
\begin{array}{lllll}
\textsf{(A)} \: 250 &
\textsf{(B)} \: 275 &
\textsf{(C)} \: 392 &
\textsf{(D)} \: 401 &
\textsf{(E)} \: 500 & \\
\end{array}
$  
:::
#### Solution
![Question proportions and percentages figure Q-4 solve-1.svg](../../public/math/Core%20Courses/Question%20proportions%20and%20percentages%20figure%20Q-4%20solve-1.svg)  
::: wrap  
$
\begin{array}{ll}
1\frac{1}{3}=\frac{3}{3}+\frac{1}{3}=\frac{4}{3} \\
\textsf{male employees}=\frac{4}{3}\cdot\textsf{female employees}=420 \\
\textsf{female employees}=\frac{3}{4}\cdot420=\frac{1260}{4}=315 \\
x\gt20=20\% \\
\left.
\begin{array}{cc}
10\le x\le20 \\
x\lt10 \\
\end{array}
\right\}80\% \\
10\le x\le20:x\lt10 = 1:2 \Longrightarrow \frac{1}{3}:\frac{2}{3} \\
x\lt10=735\cdot80\%\cdot\frac{2}{3}=\frac{735\cdot0.8\cdot2}{3}=\frac{1176}{3}=392 \\
\end{array}
$  
:::  
#### Conclusion
- Derived Solution  
  ::: wrap
  > $\boldsymbol{(C)}$  
  > According to the Solution, get $x\lt10=392$, so choose $C$. 

  :::

## 4.Focus 4
__The variable ratio problems (involving a constant quantity)__  
- The standard method is to __set unknown variables and solve__,
  while __a more concise method is to use the combined parts of the constant quantity__,
  analyze __the parts of the changing quantities__,
  and then find __the value of each part__.

### $\fbox{5}\:$The ratio of the amount of bread stored in warehouses $\small A$ and $\small B$ is $\small 4:3$. Now, $\small 10\textsf{K}$ of bread is moved from warehouse $\small A$. After the transfer, the ratio of the bread weight in warehouses $\small A$ and $\small B$ becomes $7:6$. Find the original amount of bread in warehouse $\small A$.
::: wrap  
$
\begin{array}{lllll}
\textsf{(A)} \: 70\textsf{K tons} &
\textsf{(B)} \: 78\textsf{K tons}  &
\textsf{(C)} \: 80\textsf{K tons}  &
\textsf{(D)} \: 85\textsf{K tons}  &
\textsf{(E)} \: 88\textsf{K tons}  & \\
\end{array}
$  
:::
#### Solution
::: wrap  
$
\begin{array}{ll}
B=\textsf{constant quantity} \\
A:B=4:3=8:6 & \textsf{before}\\
A:B=7:6=7:6 & \textsf{after} \\
8\textsf{ parts}-7\textsf{ parts}=1\textsf{ part} \\
1\textsf{ part}=10\textsf{K} \\
A_\textsf{origin}=8\cdot10\textsf{K}=80\textsf{K} \\
\end{array}
$  
:::  
#### Conclusion
- Derived Solution  
  ::: wrap
  > $\boldsymbol{(C)}$  
  > According to the Solution, get $A_\textsf{before}=80\textsf{K}$, so choose $C$. 

  :::

### $\fbox{6}\:$There are $\small 108$ people running on the playground. Among them, girls make up $\small \frac{2}{9}$ of the total. After some more girls arrive, the number of girls becomes $\small \frac{3}{7}$ of the number of boys. Find how many girls arrived.
::: wrap  
$
\begin{array}{lllll}
\textsf{(A)} \: 6 &
\textsf{(B)} \: 8 &
\textsf{(C)} \: 10 &
\textsf{(D)} \: 12 &
\textsf{(E)} \: 15 & \\
\end{array}
$  
:::
#### Solution
::: wrap  
$
\begin{array}{ll}
\textsf{boys}=\textsf{constant quantity} \\
\textsf{boys}:\textsf{girls}=7:2 & \textsf{before} \\
\textsf{boys}:\textsf{girls}=7:3 & \textsf{after} \\
3\textsf{ parts}-2\textsf{ parts}=1\textsf{ part} \\
9\textsf{ parts}=108 \\
1\textsf{ part}=\frac{108}{9}=12 \\
\textsf{girls}_\textsf{arrive}=1=12 \\
\end{array}
$  
:::  
#### Conclusion
- Derived Solution  
  ::: wrap
  > $\boldsymbol{(D)}$  
  > According to the Solution, get $\textsf{girls}_\textsf{arrive}=12$, so choose $D$. 

  :::
